Subject: Critical Out-of-Specification (OOS) Defect & Discoloration - Metformin HCI 500mg Tablets (Batch #FDF-9082-X)

Dear Quality Team,

We are writing to register an urgent quality complaint regarding our recent shipment of Metformin Hydrochloride 500mg Finished Dosage Form (FDF) Film-Coated Tablets received under Purchase Order #PO-88219.

Incident Details:
- Customer Name: MetroHealth Global Logistics / St. Jude Regional Hospital Network
- Product Name: Metformin HCl 500mg Film-Coated Tablets (FDF)
- Product Strength / Grade: 500 mg USP Grade
- Batch / Lot Number: Batch #FDF-9082-X
- Manufacturing Date: 2025-11-10
- Expiry Date: 2028-11-09
- Quantity Affected: 25,000 Blister Packs (approx. 250,000 tablets)
- Complaint Date: 2026-07-19

Defect Description:
During routine incoming quality inspection at our primary hospital dispensary, our technical staff observed significant mottling and dark brown discoloration on approximately 18% of the inspected tablets. Furthermore, three blister foils showed seal integrity breaches leading to tablet crumbling. Chemical assay preliminary testing revealed an Out-of-Specification (OOS) dissolution rate of only 68% at 45 minutes (USP specification requires >= 80%).

Risk & Impact Assessment:
This defect directly impacts patient safety and therapeutic efficacy. We have quarantined the entire lot immediately. Please initiate a formal Root Cause Analysis (RCA), sample retrieval, and CAPA under cGMP Guidelines.
